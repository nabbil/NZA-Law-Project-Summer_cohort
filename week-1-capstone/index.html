Paul Bozych and Preetha Jayakumar won motions to dismiss (TCPA).

The rulings reflect a significant departure from the Seventh Circuit case law concerning alleged TCPA statutory violations and Article III standing. In both suits, the plaintiffs alleged that the defendants violated the TCPA by sending unsolicited facsimile advertisements that do not comply with the TCPA’s opt-out notice requirements. The TCPA permits a recipient of such fax advertisements to bring legal action against the sender and recover either actual damages or statutory damages up to $1500 per violation. The 7th Circuit courts have generally held that receipt of unsolicited fax advertisement with an imperfect opt-out notice is sufficient to confer an Article III injury. However, the recent rulings clarified that the receipt of such a fax absent a de facto injury is insufficient as a matter of law to establish Article III standing under the Supreme Court’s holding in Spokeo v. Robins, 136 S. C.t. 1540 (2016).
